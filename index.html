Usability Test Report

Summary

The group conducted a workshop consisting of two sessions: Programming Workshop and Cyber-bullying Workshop. Due to the ongoing pandemic of the Coronavirus 19, the tests were taken remotely. The group used a web application, which is Google Forms, for us to conduct the Usability Test and for us to collect the results of the test. The Usability Test was conducted after the two sessions in order for the audience to give feedback to the conducted workshops.

Goals

The goal of the team behind the usability test is to find out the feedback from the participants and what the group members lack as a group. This is for the group to improve the workshop and to teach the participants about the chosen topics.

Methodology

The group provided 6 survey questions before the execution of the workshop, and after the workshop, the link was shared to the audiences through Messenger. After the test audience answered the survey, the team tallied the responses of the respondents. Then, the group made a graph, individual responses, and tallying of results of their responses in a Google Sheets file.

Participants

The test audience of the workshop are students. The test audience of the workshop are mostly female. The age of most of the participants are 14 years old. Some participants have knowledge about web development. Some participants have experienced cyberbullying.

Findings

The team found out that one of the two critical issues encountered during the workshop is the utilization of the speakers. The team was not able to plan who will be the substitute speakers if one of the members disconnects. The other critical issue was the team did not have a smooth transition in between the workshops. The team was unable to execute a dry-run to practice what is needed, such as the transition, since the team will be lacking time if a dry-run is to be done.
